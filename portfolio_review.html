Finding summary

Before Q1 2024, Order exhibited no measurable concentration of payments in the 45–60 day window past due — fewer than 0.02% of paid loans in that range, with funds arriving a median of 56 days before the due date. This is consistent with a pass-through servicer releasing funds promptly on receipt.

From April 2024 onwards, 10.5% of paid loans arrived in the 45–60 day window — a ∞x increase. The average DPD at payoff moved from -54.9d to 13.6d. The peak month was 2025-09-01 at 52.4%. This pattern is statistically inconsistent with obligor payment behavior and consistent with the servicer batching collected funds and remitting at or near our maturity date, capturing float in the interim.

Separately, payments landing on the exact partner due date (DPD=0) are 3.29× more frequent than the statistical expectation from adjacent days — a second independent signal of batch remittance behavior.

Conservative float income estimate at 5% annual over 52.4 average hold days: $325K. At prevailing SOFR+ rates during the period, the likely range is $325K–$813K on $45.3M principal across 90,593 loans.
